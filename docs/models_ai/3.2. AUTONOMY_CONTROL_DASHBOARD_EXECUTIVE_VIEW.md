## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document defines the **executive control plane for autonomous operations governance**. It specifies the information, controls, and accountability mechanisms that executives require to govern autonomous behavior across the enterprise system.

**This is not an operational dashboard.**  
**This is not a performance dashboard.**  
**This is an executive control plane.**

**Core Purpose:**  
The dashboard must answer ONE question instantly: **"Is autonomy currently helping or hurting the business?"**

If the answer is unclear, the dashboard has failed.

**Design Principles:**
1. **Risk > Performance** â€” Show risk exposure before efficiency gains
2. **Signal > Volume** â€” Show critical deviations, not comprehensive data
3. **Exceptions > Averages** â€” Show what requires attention, not what is normal
4. **Control > Automation** â€” Provide kill switches and override mechanisms
5. **Fewer widgets, deeper meaning** â€” Every element must inform a decision

**Usage:**
- **Daily review:** CTO, COO
- **Weekly review:** CFO, Chief Risk Officer, Risk Committee
- **Monthly review:** CEO
- **Quarterly review:** Board Audit Committee

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### What This Dashboard Is

- **A control plane** for governing autonomous operations
- **A risk visibility tool** showing where autonomy may create harm
- **A decision support system** enabling executives to intervene
- **An accountability mechanism** logging who approved what and why

### What This Dashboard Is NOT

- **Not a celebration tool** showing how much autonomy is active
- **Not an operational metrics dashboard** (orders/day, revenue trends)
- **Not an AI performance showcase** (accuracy percentages without context)
- **Not a data exploration platform** (no drill-down into raw transactions)

### The Single Question

Every element on this dashboard must contribute to answering:

**"Is autonomy currently helping or hurting the business?"**

- **Helping:** Autonomy is stable, overrides are low, no incidents, risk contained
- **Hurting:** Autonomy is drifting, overrides increasing, incidents occurring, risk escalating
- **Unclear:** Data is ambiguous or missing â†’ Dashboard failure, require investigation

## SECTION 3: AUTONOMY DRIFT & CONFIDENCE INDEX

### 3.1 Purpose

Provide **aggregate health metrics** that synthesize autonomy performance across all domains. These metrics are **executive-only** and not visible to operational teams.

### 3.2 Metric 1: Autonomy Confidence Score

#### Definition

A **composite score (0-100)** representing overall confidence that autonomous operations are performing as expected.

#### Calculation Components

| Component | Weight | Measurement |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Historical Accuracy** | 40% | % of autonomous actions that matched expected outcome (last 90 days) |
| **Override Rate** | 30% | Inverse of override % (lower overrides = higher confidence) |
| **Business Impact Deviation** | 20% | Inverse of deviation from expected business outcomes (stockout rate, margin, etc.) |
| **Time Since Last Incident** | 10% | Days since last Moderate/Major/Critical incident (capped at 90 days) |

#### Score Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation | Executive Action |
|-------|----------------|------------------|
| **90-100** | Excellent: Autonomy highly trustworthy | Maintain, consider expansion |
| **75-89** | Good: Autonomy mostly reliable | Maintain, monitor trends |
| **60-74** | Fair: Autonomy requires attention | Investigate low performers, tune thresholds |
| **40-59** | Poor: Autonomy losing trust | Downgrade high-risk capabilities, enhance safeguards |
| **0-39** | Failing: Autonomy creating harm | Emergency review, consider suspension |

#### Thresholds

- **Confidence <60:** Automatic executive review triggered (CTO + COO)
- **Confidence <40:** Automatic downgrade of all Level 3+ autonomy to Level 2

## SECTION 6: AUTONOMY KILL SWITCHES (EXECUTIVE ONLY)

### 6.1 Purpose

Provide **immediate control mechanisms** for executives to suspend autonomy when trust is lost or risk escalates.

### 6.2 Kill Switch Types

#### Switch 6A: Global Autonomy Suspend

**Action:** Suspends ALL Level 3+ autonomy across all domains

**Effect:**
- All Level 3 (auto-action) and Level 4 (self-tuning) capabilities immediately downgraded to Level 1 (recommendation only)
- Level 2 (auto-action with approval) remains active (requires human approval anyway)

**Use case:** Critical incident, system-wide trust failure, regulatory emergency

**Execution time:** <60 seconds from button press to full suspension

---

#### Switch 6B: Per-Domain Suspend

**Action:** Suspends autonomy for specific domain only

**Effect:**
- Selected domain: All Level 3+ capabilities downgraded to Level 1
- Other domains: Unaffected

**Use case:** Domain-specific incident or performance degradation

**Execution time:** <60 seconds

---

#### Switch 6C: Per-Capability Suspend

**Action:** Suspends specific autonomous capability

**Effect:**
- Selected capability: Downgraded to Level 0 (manual only) or Level 1 (recommendation)
- Other capabilities: Unaffected

**Use case:** Targeted intervention for problematic capability

**Execution time:** <60 seconds

#### Resume Requires Explicit Confirmation

**Suspension is NOT automatically reversed.**

1. **Root cause addressed:** Issue that triggered suspension must be resolved
2. **Performance validated:** Evidence that autonomy will perform better (testing, logic fixes)
3. **Executive approval:** Same or higher authority must approve resumption
4. **Pilot period:** Resume with 30-day enhanced monitoring

**Resume is harder than suspend.** By design.

## CLOSING STATEMENT

Autonomy is powerful when governed. Autonomy is dangerous when invisible.

This dashboard exists to ensure that **executives remain in control** even as systems become more autonomous. It provides:

- **Visibility** into what autonomy is active and how it is performing
- **Control** through kill switches and tier adjustments
- **Accountability** through decision logging and board reporting

The goal is **not** to maximize autonomy. The goal is to **maximize trust in autonomy**.

Trust requires:
- Transparency (executives see what is happening)
- Control (executives can intervene instantly)
- Accountability (decisions are logged and reviewed)

Without this dashboard, autonomy becomes a black box. With this dashboard, autonomy becomes a governed capability.

**Autonomy exists to reduce cognitive load, not to remove accountability.**

Executives delegate execution to systems, but they **never delegate accountability** for outcomes.

When autonomous systems act, executives remain accountable. This dashboard ensures they have the visibility and control to fulfill that accountability.
