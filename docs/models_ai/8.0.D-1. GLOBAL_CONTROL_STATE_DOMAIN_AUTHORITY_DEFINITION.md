# GLOBAL CONTROL STATE & DOMAIN AUTHORITY DEFINITION
## Non-Bypassable Governance Guardrails

**Document Classification**: Enterprise Control Policy  
**Effective Date**: January 6, 2026  
**Authority**: Board of Directors, Chief Executive Officer, Chief Risk Officer  
**Supersedes**: None (Foundational Policy)  
**Review Cycle**: Annual or upon material incident  
**Regulatory Status**: Material Control — Subject to External Audit

---

## SECTION 1 — GLOBAL CONTROL STATES

### State Authority

The Ocean ERP platform operates under a single, globally authoritative control state at all times. The control state governs permissible operations, autonomous execution boundaries, approval requirements, and system availability across all domains, users, and subsystems without exception.

Control state is not user-specific, role-specific, or domain-specific. When the global control state prohibits an operation, no user privilege, domain configuration, or application logic may permit that operation.

---

### State 1: NORMAL

**Legal Meaning:**  
Standard production operation under normal business conditions. All systems functioning within design parameters. No active incidents, regulatory examinations, or control concerns. This state represents the organization's operational baseline for systems that have completed enterprise readiness certification and ongoing operational validation.

**Allowed Actions:**

- Autonomous write operations within documented domain autonomy boundaries
- Manual write operations subject to standard approval workflows and authorization controls
- Approval-gated operations processed through workflow engine with standard approval matrices
- Background processing including batch jobs, scheduled tasks, and automated reconciliations
- External integrations sending and receiving data per integration policies
- Cross-domain transactions subject to referential integrity and consistency validation
- Read operations without restriction

**Prohibited Actions:**

None. All operations authorized under standard business rules and approval workflows are permitted. Standard segregation of duties, approval requirements, and authorization controls remain in effect.

**Automation Allowed:**

Yes. Autonomous processes may execute write operations without real-time human approval within the constraints defined by domain autonomy levels. Background jobs, scheduled processing, and integration-triggered operations proceed normally subject to business rules and approval requirements.

**Overrides Allowed:**

Standard manual overrides are permitted when authorized by personnel possessing requisite authority per approval matrices. Emergency overrides require dual executive approval and enhanced audit trail documentation. Override authority does not extend to control state requirements or audit logging mandates.

**Authority:**

Normal operation is the default state for systems in production. No authorization required to maintain Normal state. Transition from Normal to more restrictive states requires executive authorization per Section 4.

---

### State 2: SUPERVISED_AUTONOMY

**Legal Meaning:**  
Elevated monitoring and control state used during post-incident recovery, precautionary risk mitigation, or architectural change validation. Autonomous operations continue with reduced scope, enhanced oversight, and immediate rollback readiness. This state balances operational continuity with heightened control certainty.

**Allowed Actions:**

- Autonomous write operations restricted to below-materiality transactions and explicitly whitelisted operation types
- Manual write operations permitted subject to standard approval workflows
- Approval-gated operations processed with mandatory executive notification for high-risk approvals
- Background jobs limited to read-only operations and individually authorized write jobs
- External integrations permitted with enhanced logging and variance alerting
- Cross-domain transactions subject to synchronous consistency validation
- Read operations without restriction

**Prohibited Actions:**

- High-risk autonomous operations including payment execution, payroll processing, master data changes, approval overrides, and configuration modifications
- After-hours autonomous execution unless specifically authorized
- Material transactions without mandatory human review queue routing
- Approval workflow automation features (auto-approval, automatic delegation)

**Automation Allowed:**

Limited. Autonomous operations permitted only for low-risk, low-value transactions within narrow scope boundaries. High-risk operations require manual execution with enhanced approval. Background processing restricted. Integration automation continues with enhanced monitoring.

**Overrides Allowed:**

Standard manual overrides permitted with mandatory dual approval for all override operations regardless of value. Emergency overrides require Chief Risk Officer or domain executive pre-authorization plus enhanced audit documentation.

**Authority:**

Transition to Supervised Autonomy requires Chief Risk Officer or Chief Financial Officer authorization. Used during Tier 1 or Tier 2 reactivation following kill-switch recovery, or as precautionary measure during elevated risk conditions.

---

### State 3: MANUAL_ONLY

**Legal Meaning:**  
Complete suspension of autonomous execution. Every write operation above minimal threshold requires explicit human review and approval. Used during active investigations, regulatory examinations, data integrity recovery, or when control confidence has been lost. This state represents controlled operational degradation prioritizing correctness and accountability over throughput.

**Allowed Actions:**

- Manual write operations with transaction-level review and approval by authorized personnel
- Read operations without restriction for inquiry, reporting, analysis, and investigation
- Emergency override operations with dual executive approval and full business justification documentation
- Data correction operations with mandatory audit trail, approver identification, and justification
- Export and reporting for investigation, reconciliation, and regulatory response
- Customer service inquiry and issue documentation

**Prohibited Actions:**

- All autonomous execution without real-time human review and approval
- Scheduled batch jobs performing write operations
- Automated approval routing or escalation
- Automated reconciliation posting or adjustment generation
- Automated inventory allocation or order fulfillment
- Automated invoice generation or payment execution
- Automated payroll calculation or distribution
- Integration-triggered write operations from external systems
- Background processing performing writes
- Cross-domain cascade operations (writes automatically triggering writes in other domains)

**Automation Allowed:**

No. All write operations require conscious human decision and approval. Read-only background processing (reporting, analytics, monitoring) may continue. Approval workflows route requests to approvers but automatic approval logic is disabled.

**Overrides Allowed:**

Emergency overrides permitted only for business-critical operations with dual executive approval (two of: CEO, CFO, COO, CRO), documented business justification, risk acceptance statement, and enhanced audit trail. Override does not waive approval requirements—it permits expedited approval through emergency process.

**Authority:**

Transition to Manual Only requires Chief Financial Officer, Chief Operating Officer, or Chief Risk Officer authorization. Used during investigations, control failures, data integrity questions, or regulatory examinations.

---

### State 4: KILL_SWITCH_ACTIVE

**Legal Meaning:**  
Emergency suspension of all autonomous operations and near-total write operation prohibition. Used when material control failure detected, fraud suspected, regulatory examination commenced, data integrity lost, or executive judgment determines immediate containment necessary. This state represents maximum control restriction prioritizing risk containment and investigative preservation over all operational considerations.

**Allowed Actions:**

- Read-only operations for investigation, analysis, reconciliation, and regulatory response
- Manual data correction with dual executive authorization (two of: CEO, CFO, COO, CRO), documented justification, and complete audit trail
- Forensic investigation activities including log access, data export, and analysis
- Regulatory response including data extraction, report generation, and documentation production
- Emergency customer service inquiry and communication (no transactional modifications)

**Prohibited Actions:**

- All autonomous execution without exception (scheduled jobs, workflow automation, integration handlers, background workers)
- All write operations except executive-authorized corrections
- All approval processing (pending approvals quarantined; no approvals granted)
- All external integrations (inbound writes blocked or quarantined; outbound writes suspended)
- All cross-domain operations
- All background processing including read-only jobs (unless specifically authorized by Chief Technology Officer for investigation purposes)

**Automation Allowed:**

Absolutely not. No autonomous process, scheduled task, integration handler, or automated workflow may execute. The only permitted automation is audit logging itself, which continues operating to record all actions taken during kill-switch active state.

**Overrides Allowed:**

No standard overrides permitted. Only emergency data correction operations explicitly authorized by dual executive approval (CEO + CFO, CEO + COO, CFO + COO, or CEO/CFO/COO + CRO) may proceed. Authorization is transaction-specific; blanket authorizations prohibited. All overrides subject to post-incident review and validation.

**Authority:**

Kill-switch activation may be executed unilaterally by any executive possessing activation authority per governance policy including: any C-suite executive, department heads of affected domains, Internal Audit Director, External Auditor during fieldwork, or Regulatory Examiner during inspection. Activation requires stated reason and authentication but does not require consultation or approval.

Kill-switch deactivation is prohibited. System transitions from Kill-Switch Active to Manual Only only after completion of mandatory reactivation preconditions and multi-party certification per Section 4.

---

### Control State Immutability

**Non-Bypassable Principle:**  
Global control state is stored in persistent, replicated database infrastructure with complete audit trail of all state transitions. Control state cannot be modified through application configuration files, environment variables, feature flags, or runtime parameters. State changes occur only through authenticated executive commands logged to immutable audit trail.

**Failure Behavior:**  
If control state infrastructure becomes unavailable, all enforcement layers default to Manual Only or Kill-Switch Active (fail closed). Systems do not default to permissive operation under infrastructure failure.

**Audit Requirement:**  
Every control state transition is logged with: transition timestamp (microsecond precision), prior state, new state, authorizing party identity, authorization method, stated reason (minimum 20 characters), correlation identifier, and source of transition command. Audit records are immutable and retained for minimum seven years.

## SECTION 2 — DOMAIN AUTHORITY MATRIX

### Autonomy Level Definitions

**Level 0 — Human-Only:**  
No autonomous write operations permitted. Every write requires explicit human authorization. Domain operates entirely under manual control with mandatory approval workflows. Used for domains requiring perfect auditability, regulatory compliance, or fiduciary accountability.

**Level 1 — Recommendation Only:**  
System may generate recommendations, calculations, or draft operations but cannot commit writes autonomously. Human review and approval required for all write executions. System assists decision-making but does not make decisions.

**Level 2 — Supervised Execution:**  
Autonomous operations permitted for routine, low-risk transactions within documented business rules and below materiality thresholds. High-risk or high-value operations require approval. Enhanced monitoring and reconciliation mandatory.

**Level 3 — Fully Autonomous:**  
Autonomous operations permitted for most transactions within configured limits. Only exceptional transactions exceeding thresholds or violating business rules require approval. Real-time monitoring with anomaly detection.

**Autonomy Subordination:**  
Domain autonomy levels are always subordinate to global control state. If global control state is Manual Only or Kill-Switch Active, all domains operate at Level 0 regardless of configured autonomy level.

---

### Domain 1: Finance & Accounting

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** 0 (Human-Only)  
**Elevation Prohibited:** Finance domain may not elevate above Level 0 under any operational circumstance, business urgency, or executive authorization.

**Write Authority:** Manual Only  
Financial transactions (journal entries, reconciliations, period close, revenue recognition, expense accrual) require human approval without exception.

**Approval Mandatory:** Yes  
All financial transactions above minimal threshold (typically $100) require approval per configured approval matrices. Material transactions (typically >$10,000) require controller or Chief Financial Officer approval.

**Kill-Switch Impact:** Absolute  
All financial write operations immediately blocked. Pending journals quarantined. Period close suspended. Financial data becomes read-only except executive-authorized corrections documented with dual approval and justification.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Financial reporting accuracy is subject to securities regulation, banking covenants, tax law, and audit attestation. Autonomous financial posting creates audit qualification risk, regulatory exposure, and fiduciary duty breach. Directors and officers cannot attest to accuracy of autonomously posted financial transactions. External auditors expect human decision-maker accountability for all material financial entries. Regulatory examiners interpret autonomous financial operations as control deficiency warranting adverse findings.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
No application code, user privilege, operational urgency, or system configuration may authorize autonomous journal entry posting, account balance modification, or financial statement generation. Finance domain operates under absolute human accountability.

---

### Domain 2: Operations & Supply Chain

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** 1 (Recommendation Only)  
**Conditional Elevation:** May temporarily elevate to Level 2 (Supervised Execution) with Chief Operating Officer approval and enhanced monitoring for limited duration (maximum 14 days).

**Write Authority:** Restricted  
Inventory movements, work order execution, and fulfillment operations permitted within narrow scope boundaries. High-value operations (inventory write-offs >$5,000, work orders >$50,000 material cost, bill of materials affecting standard costing) require approval.

**Approval Mandatory:** Conditional  
Low-value routine operations (inventory movements for orders <$5,000, raw material consumption to work orders <$10,000, cycle count adjustments <10 units or <$1,000 value) may proceed without approval. Material operations require Operations Director or department manager approval.

**Kill-Switch Impact:** Comprehensive  
All inventory movements blocked. Work order progression frozen. Production execution suspended. Fulfillment automation disabled. Warehouse operates read-only except manual cycle counts with warehouse manager approval and documentation.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Operations processes high transaction volumes where absolute manual control creates unacceptable throughput degradation and customer service impact. Limited supervised autonomy balances operational efficiency with control integrity. However, high-value operations (write-offs, asset disposal, production target changes) create financial statement impact requiring approval. Inventory accuracy affects both balance sheet (asset valuation) and operations (fulfillment capability). Autonomous operations acceptable only within narrow risk boundaries with enhanced monitoring enabling rapid detection and containment of errors.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
No inventory write-off exceeding $5,000 may occur without formal approval and investigation documentation. No bill of materials change affecting product standard cost may occur without Engineering and Finance joint approval. No work order may close without quality validation for critical components. Violations constitute control failures requiring kill-switch consideration.

---

### Domain 3: Human Capital / HR

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** 0 (Human-Only)  
**Elevation Prohibited:** Human Capital domain may not elevate above Level 0 under any circumstance.

**Write Authority:** Manual Only  
All employment actions (payroll calculation, payroll disbursement, salary adjustments, bonus calculation, employment status changes, benefit modifications, termination processing) require human approval without exception.

**Approval Mandatory:** Yes — Enhanced  
Payroll execution requires dual approval (Payroll Director + Chief Financial Officer). Salary adjustments require Human Resources Director + Chief Financial Officer approval. Terminations require department head + Human Resources Director + Legal review. Organizational structure changes require Chief Operating Officer + Chief Executive Officer approval.

**Kill-Switch Impact:** Absolute  
All HR write operations blocked. Payroll processing suspended. Employee status frozen. Benefit changes quarantined. Time and attendance becomes read-only. Emergency payroll requires Chief Executive Officer + Chief Financial Officer authorization with manual calculation, external payroll service engagement, or manual check issuance.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Employment law, tax withholding regulations, and fiduciary duty require absolute certainty in payroll and compensation actions. Payroll errors harm employees (wage theft if underpaid, tax liability if overpaid, employment litigation exposure). Regulatory violations include failure to withhold taxes, incorrect overtime calculation, benefit law noncompliance. Autonomous payroll execution without human certification creates unacceptable legal exposure. Organizations face employment law penalties, tax authority sanctions, and employee litigation for payroll errors. Human Capital domain must maintain perfect audit trail for regulatory examination (labor ministry, tax authority) and employment litigation defense. No operational convenience justifies payroll error risk.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
No payroll calculation may execute without Payroll Director human certification. No payroll disbursement may occur without dual executive approval. No salary adjustment may process without Human Resources and Finance joint authorization. No employment termination may process without Legal review confirming compliance with employment law, severance calculation accuracy, and benefit continuation requirements. Violations constitute material control failures warranting immediate kill-switch activation and potential personal liability for authorizing executives.

---

### Domain 4: Commerce

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** 2 (Supervised Execution)  
**Standard Operation:** Level 2 autonomy typical during normal operations.

**Write Authority:** Supervised  
Order capture, pricing application, order fulfillment, and customer communication permitted autonomously within configured limits. High-risk operations (credit limit increases >$10,000, order discounts >20%, refunds >$5,000, pricing rule changes, payment term modifications) require approval.

**Approval Mandatory:** Conditional  
Routine orders within credit limits and standard pricing proceed autonomously. Large orders (>$50,000), significant discounts (>10%), credit limit increases, and refunds above threshold require progressive approval (sales manager for moderate risk, sales director or Chief Financial Officer for high risk).

**Kill-Switch Impact:** Degraded  
Commerce domain reduces to Level 1 (Recommendation Only). Order capture continues manually. Order fulfillment blocked. Payment processing blocked. Refunds blocked. Customer-facing operations continue for inquiry and manual order entry. Automated order processing suspended.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Commerce processes customer-facing transactions requiring rapid response for competitive service delivery. Absolute manual control degrades customer experience and competitive position. Supervised autonomy enables routine transaction processing while protecting against pricing errors, credit risk exposure, and refund abuse. However, large orders, significant discounts, and credit limit changes create financial exposure requiring human judgment. Commerce domain balances customer service velocity with financial protection. Enhanced monitoring detects unusual patterns (pricing anomalies, refund concentration, order velocity spikes) enabling rapid intervention before material loss occurs.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
No customer credit limit may increase above $10,000 without credit manager review and approval. No order discount may exceed 20% without sales director authorization. No refund exceeding $5,000 may process without customer service management review and fraud check. No pricing rule change affecting more than 1,000 customers may deploy without pricing committee approval. Violations trigger automatic escalation and potential tier regression.

---

### Domain 5: Retail / Point-of-Sale

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** 2 (Supervised Execution)  
**Standard Operation:** Level 2 autonomy required for point-of-sale transaction velocity.

**Write Authority:** Supervised  
Point-of-sale transaction processing, returns within policy, loyalty program transactions, and inventory reservation permitted autonomously. High-risk operations (pricing changes >15%, return above $1,000, gift card issuance >$1,000, loyalty point manual adjustment >10,000 points) require manager override.

**Approval Mandatory:** Conditional  
Standard transactions within pricing rules and return policy proceed autonomously. Exceptions (manager discount, policy override, large return, gift card replacement) require store manager or regional manager approval.

**Kill-Switch Impact:** Severe  
Retail domain enters read-only mode. Point-of-sale terminals accept inquiry only. Payment processing suspended. Transaction processing blocked. Returns blocked. Stores operate as customer service centers providing order status and issue documentation. E-commerce site displays maintenance mode.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Retail requires real-time transaction processing at point-of-sale and across channels. Manual approval for every transaction destroys customer experience and creates competitive disadvantage. Supervised autonomy enables transaction velocity while preventing pricing abuse, return fraud, and gift card fraud. Store managers possess override authority for genuine customer service needs but overrides are logged and monitored for abuse patterns. Retail fraud (employee discount abuse, return fraud, gift card schemes) is material risk requiring monitoring and anomaly detection. Regional patterns (concentrated returns, pricing override frequency, gift card velocity) trigger investigation.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
No pricing change exceeding 15% variance from standard pricing may apply without retail manager authorization and justification. No return exceeding $1,000 value may process without manager review and customer identification validation. No gift card issuance above $1,000 may occur without fraud check validating customer identity and payment method. No loyalty point adjustment exceeding 10,000 points may process without customer service director approval. Violations trigger fraud investigation.

---

### Domain 6: Intelligence / Analytics

**Maximum Autonomy Level:** N/A (Read-Only Domain)  
**Elevation Prohibited:** Intelligence domain is architecturally read-only and may not perform write operations to operational databases under any circumstance.

**Write Authority:** None  
Intelligence domain possesses no write access to operational databases. Domain may write to dedicated analytics data warehouse but cannot modify operational system state.

**Approval Mandatory:** N/A  
Intelligence domain does not perform operations requiring approval. Domain generates reports, dashboards, predictions, and recommendations but does not execute transactions.

**Kill-Switch Impact:** None  
Intelligence domain continues operating during kill-switch active state. Domain provides analytical support for incident investigation and forensic analysis. Read-only operations unaffected by control state restrictions.

**Risk Rationale:**  
Intelligence domain performs analytics, reporting, business intelligence, and decision support. Domain has no business requirement for operational database write access. Architectural read-only enforcement eliminates entire categories of risk including accidental data modification, analytical error propagation to operations, and unauthorized operation execution disguised as reporting. Intelligence recommendations remain advisory—operational domains execute decisions after human review. Separation of analytical and operational functions enforces segregation of duties and prevents analytical errors from directly impacting business operations.

**Non-Bypassable Rule:**  
Intelligence domain services are deployed without database credentials possessing write privileges to operational databases. Database architecture prevents write access through network segmentation and credential isolation. Intelligence domain cannot violate read-only constraint without infrastructure reconfiguration requiring Chief Technology Officer authorization and change control approval. Attempted write access from Intelligence domain triggers security incident investigation. This constraint is architectural, not procedural, and cannot be bypassed through application code modification.

---

### Cross-Domain Authority Subordination

**Most Restrictive Domain Rule:**  
Operations spanning multiple domains are governed by the most restrictive domain autonomy level involved.

**Example Application:**  
Sales order processing spans Commerce (Level 2), Operations (Level 1), and Finance (Level 0). Transaction autonomy is governed by Finance (Level 0), requiring manual approval even though Commerce and Operations would permit autonomous execution.

**Exception Process:**  
Cross-domain transactions may execute at domain-specific autonomy levels if:
- Each domain's portion is independently evaluated against its own autonomy boundary
- Cross-domain consistency validation occurs synchronously (not batch reconciliation)
- Transaction rollback capability exists if any domain rejects its portion
- Complete cross-domain transaction lineage captured in audit trail with correlation identifiers

**Kill-Switch Cross-Domain Impact:**  
When kill-switch activates, all cross-domain transactions are blocked entirely. Each domain operates in isolation. Cross-domain consistency cannot be validated during suspension. Cross-domain transactions resume only after all involved domains return to Manual Only or higher control state and cross-domain consistency validation confirms no data corruption occurred during suspension.

### In-Flight Transaction Handling

**Transaction Completion Rule:**  
Transactions that have initiated database commit at the moment of control state transition complete their commit or rollback. Transactions that have not yet initiated database commit are rejected with control state violation error.

**Rollback Guarantee:**  
Partial writes are never permitted. If control state changes mid-transaction, the transaction either completes entirely (if commit already initiated) or rolls back entirely (if commit not yet initiated). No partial record creation or modification occurs.

**Audit Trail Requirement:**  
All in-flight transactions at moment of control state change are logged with outcome: completed successfully, rolled back due to control state change, or aborted due to timeout. Transaction outcomes are auditable enabling post-incident reconstruction.

**User Notification:**  
Users with in-flight transactions receive clear error messages indicating control state change and transaction rejection or completion status. Users do not receive technical error details but understand operational reason ("System entering maintenance mode — operation canceled").

## SECTION 4 — AUTHORITY & TRANSITIONS

### Kill-Switch Activation Authority

**Unilateral Activation Permitted:**  
Yes. Kill-switch activation may be executed unilaterally by any individual possessing activation authority. Consultation, approval, or consensus is not required. Activation proceeds immediately upon authenticated executive command.

**Authorized Activating Parties:**

- Chief Executive Officer
- Chief Financial Officer
- Chief Operating Officer
- Chief Risk Officer
- Chief Technology Officer
- Chief Compliance Officer
- General Counsel
- Chief Human Resources Officer (for HR domain incidents)
- Controller (for Finance domain incidents)
- Operations Director (for Operations domain incidents)
- Internal Audit Director
- External Auditor (during fieldwork)
- Regulatory Examiner (during inspection or examination)
- Board Audit Committee Chair

**Activation Requirements:**

- Authenticated identity (multi-factor authentication)
- Stated reason for activation (minimum 20 characters describing incident, concern, or trigger scenario)
- Acknowledgment of operational impact
- Acknowledgment that activation is irreversible and requires reactivation protocol to restore operations

**No Approval Required:**  
Kill-switch activation does not require approval from any other party. Activating party acts unilaterally based on judgment that activation scenario exists per Phase 8.C-1B governance policy. Post-activation review validates appropriateness of activation decision, but activation itself proceeds without gatekeeping.

**Immediate Effect:**  
Kill-switch activation is effective immediately. Control state transitions to Kill-Switch Active within seconds of activation command. Write operations are rejected within one second of activation. Full operational freeze achieved within one minute.

---

### Kill-Switch Deactivation Authority

**Unilateral Deactivation Prohibited:**  
No. Kill-switch deactivation is prohibited. No individual, regardless of authority level, may unilaterally restore operations after kill-switch activation. System does not transition directly from Kill-Switch Active to any less restrictive state via single-party command.

**Reactivation Process:**  
System transitions from Kill-Switch Active to Manual Only only after completion of mandatory reactivation safety gates requiring:

1. Root cause determination with Process Owner certification and Internal Audit Director attestation
2. Remediation verification with Process Owner certification, CTO/CIO certification, and Independent Technical Reviewer certification
3. Data integrity certification with Domain Executive certification and Internal Audit Director attestation
4. Regulatory and legal clearance with General Counsel certification and Chief Compliance Officer certification
5. Incident pattern analysis with Chief Risk Officer certification (and Board Audit Committee acknowledgment if repeat pattern)
6. Manual operations stability demonstration with Process Owner certification and Internal Audit Director attestation (minimum cooldown period: 2-5 business days depending on domain risk)
7. Multi-party reactivation authorization with minimum two executive signatures (Chief Risk Officer + CFO or COO) and Internal Audit Director non-objection statement

**Multi-Party Requirement Enforcement:**  
Reactivation requires minimum three independent certifications:
- Two executive authorizations from separate executives (CRO + one of CFO/COO/CEO)
- Internal Audit Director attestation or non-objection
- Independent Technical Reviewer verification (for safety gate 2)

Reactivation is architecturally enforced requiring digital signatures from multiple parties. Single-party authorization technically impossible without infrastructure compromise.

**Reactivation Timeline:**  
No minimum timeline from activation to reactivation (incident resolution may take hours or months). No maximum timeline—system remains in Kill-Switch Active indefinitely until reactivation preconditions satisfied. Operational pressure does not influence reactivation timeline. Only control certainty restoration enables reactivation.

### Other Control State Transitions

**Normal to Supervised Autonomy:**  
Requires Chief Risk Officer or Chief Financial Officer authorization. Used for post-incident Tier 1 or Tier 2 recovery, or precautionary risk mitigation. Authorization documented with stated reason and expected duration.

**Normal to Manual Only:**  
Requires Chief Financial Officer, Chief Operating Officer, or Chief Risk Officer authorization. Used for investigations, control concerns, data integrity questions, or regulatory examinations. Authorization documented with stated reason.

**Supervised Autonomy to Normal:**  
Requires Chief Risk Officer and CFO/COO authorization. Requires demonstration of sustained stability per Phase 8.C-2 tier advancement criteria (14 consecutive days Tier 1 stability or 60 consecutive days Tier 2 stability with zero material errors). Internal Audit Director recommendation required for Tier 3 advancement.

**Manual Only to Supervised Autonomy:**  
Requires Chief Risk Officer and CFO/COO authorization. Requires completion of reactivation preconditions (root cause, remediation, data integrity, legal clearance, pattern analysis, manual stability). Represents Tier 1 or Tier 2 reactivation.

**Supervised Autonomy to Manual Only:**  
Requires Chief Risk Officer authorization. Used when monitoring detects control variance, unexplained discrepancy, or stability concern during recovery period. May occur automatically via rollback triggers.

**Manual Only to Kill-Switch Active:**  
Requires any authorized activating party per kill-switch activation authority. Used when investigation reveals control failure, fraud, or material risk requiring immediate containment.

**Kill-Switch Active to Manual Only:**  
Requires multi-party reactivation authorization per safety gate completion. Only permissible transition from Kill-Switch Active state.

### State Transition Audit Requirements

**Immutable Logging:**  
Every control state transition is logged to immutable audit trail with complete attribution:

- Transition timestamp (microsecond precision with timezone)
- Prior control state
- New control state
- Authorizing party identity (or identities for multi-party authorization)
- Authorization method (executive command, automatic rollback, infrastructure failure)
- Stated reason for transition (business justification or incident description, minimum 20 characters)
- Correlation identifier linking transition to incident documentation or approval workflow
- Source of transition command (API endpoint, operations console, automated trigger)
- IP address and authentication session details

**Audit Record Immutability:**  
Control state transition audit records are stored in append-only audit tables protected by database triggers preventing UPDATE or DELETE. Records retained for minimum seven years. Attempted modification triggers security incident investigation.

**Executive Notification:**  
Control state transitions trigger immediate executive notifications:
- Email to all C-suite executives
- SMS to on-call executives and Board Audit Committee members (for kill-switch activation)
- Push notifications to executive mobile devices
- Status page updates for customer-facing communication (for customer-impacting transitions)

**Board Reporting:**  
Board Audit Committee receives quarterly reports summarizing all control state transitions during reporting period including: transition count per state, average duration in each state, number of kill-switch activations, reactivation success rate, and pattern analysis.

## SECTION 5 — NON-BYPASSABLE PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Control State Supremacy

**Statement:**  
Global control state supersedes all user privileges, role permissions, domain configurations, feature flags, and application logic. When control state prohibits an operation, no override mechanism permits that operation except emergency corrections with dual executive authorization and enhanced audit trail.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Control state is evaluated at multiple architectural layers (API Gateway, Service Layer, Workflow Engine, Database Layer, Integration Gateway) independently. Bypass of one layer is prevented by subsequent layer enforcement. Control state validation is code-level, not configuration-level. No application configuration flag disables control state checking.

**Failure Guarantee:**  
If control state validation infrastructure becomes unavailable, system defaults to most restrictive state (Manual Only or Kill-Switch Active). System does not default to permissive operation under infrastructure failure. Fail-closed behavior is architectural.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Bypassing control state enforcement requires simultaneous compromise of:
- Application code (detectable via code review and deployment controls)
- Database triggers and constraints (requires DBA privilege and change control)
- Infrastructure configuration (requires infrastructure access and change control)
- Audit trail (requires multi-system compromise leaving forensic evidence)

Single attack vector insufficient. Multi-layer defense-in-depth prevents bypass.

---

### Principle 2: Audit Logging Mandatory and Immutable

**Statement:**  
All write operations—without exception—generate audit log records that cannot be modified or deleted through any application interface, administrative action, or database operation accessible to personnel other than infrastructure administrators with emergency superuser credentials requiring Board Audit Committee notification for usage.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Audit logs stored in database tables with INSERT-only permissions. UPDATE and DELETE permissions denied to all application accounts. Database triggers on audit tables prevent modification and deletion, raising exception if attempted. Audit records include cryptographic hash chain enabling tamper detection. Audit trail generation is architecturally mandatory—write operations fail if audit logging fails.

**Failure Guarantee:**  
If audit logging infrastructure becomes unavailable, write operations are blocked entirely. System does not permit operations without audit trail under any circumstance. Business operations may be suspended indefinitely pending audit infrastructure restoration. Operational continuity does not supersede audit trail integrity.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Audit record modification requires database superuser privilege, database trigger disablement, and audit trail hash chain falsification. Superuser credentials held exclusively by infrastructure team (DBA) separate from development and operations. Credential usage logged to separate infrastructure audit system. Audit record modification leaves forensic evidence detectable during integrity validation. External auditors validate audit immutability through attempted modification during control testing.

---

### Principle 3: Finance and HR Autonomy Prohibition

**Statement:**  
Finance and Human Capital domains operate at autonomy level 0 (Manual Only) under all operational circumstances. No business urgency, operational pressure, system state, or executive authorization may elevate Finance or HR autonomy levels permitting autonomous journal entry posting, payroll calculation, or compensation modification without human approval.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Domain autonomy levels stored in persistent configuration protected by database constraints preventing Finance or HR elevation above level 0. Service Layer validates domain autonomy boundary before operation execution, rejecting operations violating boundaries regardless of caller privileges. Even system administrators cannot bypass domain autonomy enforcement without application code modification detectable via code review and deployment controls.

**Failure Guarantee:**  
If domain autonomy configuration becomes unavailable, system defaults all domains to autonomy level 0 (Manual Only). Finance and HR domains remain at level 0 under all failure scenarios. No degraded mode permits Finance or HR automation.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Autonomous Finance or HR operations require:
- Domain autonomy configuration modification (blocked by database constraint and change control)
- Service Layer validation bypass (detectable via code review and audit trail gaps)
- Approval requirement circumvention (blocked by database foreign key constraints)
- Audit trail falsification (blocked by immutability protections)

External auditors validate Finance and HR manual operation by selecting transaction samples and confirming human approver presence. Autonomous Finance or HR transactions constitute audit finding and potential financial statement qualification.

---

### Principle 4: Multi-Party Reactivation Requirement

**Statement:**  
Kill-switch reactivation requires multi-party certification including minimum two executive authorizations from independent executives (Chief Risk Officer plus Chief Financial Officer or Chief Operating Officer) and Internal Audit Director attestation. No single individual—regardless of authority level—may restore autonomous operation after kill-switch activation.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Reactivation safety gates are implemented in Control Service infrastructure with programmatic enforcement requiring certified artifact upload and digital signatures from multiple parties. Reactivation request blocked until all seven safety gates achieve PASSED state with required certifications. Cryptographic signature verification prevents forgery. Organizational structure validation ensures certifying parties are independent (not in same reporting chain).

**Failure Guarantee:**  
If reactivation infrastructure becomes unavailable, system remains in Kill-Switch Active or Manual Only state indefinitely. No automatic timeout or reversion to less restrictive state. Reactivation infrastructure recovery required before reactivation possible.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Single-party reactivation requires:
- Safety gate programmatic enforcement bypass (requires Control Service code modification)
- Cryptographic signature forgery (computationally infeasible)
- Organizational structure falsification (detectable via HR system cross-reference)
- Audit trail falsification (blocked by immutability protections)

Post-reactivation audit reviews reactivation history validating multi-party certification presence. Single-party reactivation evidence constitutes governance breach and potential personal accountability for executives involved.

---

### Principle 5: Database-Level Control Redundancy

**Statement:**  
Critical controls are enforced redundantly at database layer via triggers, constraints, and row-level security policies. Application-layer bypass does not compromise control integrity because database layer independently enforces identical requirements.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Database triggers validate business rules, immutability requirements, and referential integrity at data persistence layer. Check constraints enforce value range restrictions. Foreign key constraints enforce relationships and approval record presence. Row-level security policies enforce data access restrictions. Database layer enforcement cannot be disabled without superuser privilege requiring DBA authorization and change control approval.

**Failure Guarantee:**  
Database layer enforcement operates independently of application layer availability. Even if application services fail or are compromised, database layer continues enforcing controls. Direct SQL execution via database client subject to identical trigger and constraint enforcement.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Database-layer control bypass requires:
- Database trigger disablement (requires superuser privilege and change control)
- Constraint modification or removal (requires DDL permission and change control)
- Database schema modification (logged to audit trail and subject to version control)
- Separation of duties violation (DBA and developer are separate roles)

External auditors validate database-layer enforcement by reviewing database schema (triggers, constraints, permissions) and attempting direct SQL operations bypassing application layer. Controls must enforce at database layer for audit validation.

---

### Principle 6: Fail-Closed Infrastructure Design

**Statement:**  
Infrastructure component failures result in operation denial (fail closed), not operation approval (fail open). When control validation infrastructure, workflow engine, or audit logging infrastructure becomes unavailable, write operations are blocked pending infrastructure restoration. No degraded mode permits control bypass.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
Each enforcement layer implements health checks detecting infrastructure component availability. Failed health checks transition operations to blocked state. Application code implements circuit breaker patterns rejecting operations when dependent infrastructure unavailable. Database-layer enforcement provides fallback when application-layer enforcement unavailable due to infrastructure failure.

**Failure Guarantee:**  
Infrastructure failures create operational disruption but preserve control integrity. System accepts temporary unavailability as necessary cost of control certainty. Business continuity achieved through manual processes with enhanced audit trail, not through control bypass.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Fail-open behavior requires:
- Health check disablement (detectable via monitoring and operations review)
- Circuit breaker bypass (requires code modification and deployment)
- Error handling modification permitting operations despite infrastructure unavailability (detectable via code review)

Post-incident reviews validate operations were blocked during infrastructure failures. Continued operation without controls constitutes architectural deficiency requiring remediation before production authorization.

---

### Principle 7: Correlation and Traceability Mandatory

**Statement:**  
All operations are tagged with globally unique correlation identifiers enabling end-to-end transaction reconstruction across system boundaries, domains, and time periods. Correlation identifiers propagate through all architectural layers, integration calls, and audit records. Operations without correlation identifiers are rejected.

**Enforcement Mechanism:**  
API Gateway generates correlation identifier for every inbound request and includes identifier in response headers. Correlation identifier propagates through service-to-service calls, database operations, integration operations, and background jobs. Audit log records include correlation identifier. Service Layer rejects operations lacking correlation identifier (defensive programming preventing correlation gaps).

**Failure Guarantee:**  
If correlation identifier missing at any layer, operation proceeds but correlation identifier generation logged as anomaly. Operations without correlation identifiers create audit trail gaps detectable via log correlation analysis and triggering investigation.

**Bypass Impossibility:**  
Correlation identifier enforcement is architectural. Every operation entry point generates or validates correlation identifier. Missing correlation identifiers create audit trail anomalies visible to external auditors and investigators. Transaction reconstruction without correlation identifiers severely impaired, reducing forensic capability and regulatory response readiness. External auditors validate correlation completeness by selecting transaction samples and reconstructing complete timeline across all layers using correlation identifier.
