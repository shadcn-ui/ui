## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document defines the **domain-by-domain risk assessment** for autonomous operations within Ocean ERP. It establishes explicit classifications of where autonomy is safe, restricted, or forbidden, and the safeguards required for each capability.

**Core Principle:**  
Autonomy is not a binary decision. Autonomy is a **controlled gradient of risk**. Every autonomous capability must be explicitly evaluated, classified, approved, and continuously monitored.

**Purpose:**
- Prevent autonomous operations from creating uncontrolled risk
- Establish clear accountability for autonomous failures
- Provide a decision framework for evaluating autonomy requests
- Enable auditors and risk committees to assess autonomy exposure

**This document is binding.** It overrides feature ambition. It protects the enterprise from silent failure.

**No domain is autonomous by default.**

## SECTION 1: AUTONOMY RISK FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Four-Dimensional Risk Assessment

Every capability considered for autonomy must be evaluated across **four mandatory risk dimensions**:

#### Risk Dimension 1: FINANCIAL IMPACT RISK

**Definition:** The potential financial harm if autonomous action executes incorrectly or based on bad data.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Low (ðŸŸ¢):** Financial impact <$5K per action, easily reversible
- **Moderate (ðŸŸ¡):** Financial impact $5K-$50K per action, reversible with effort
- **High (ðŸŸ ):** Financial impact $50K-$500K per action, difficult to reverse
- **Critical (ðŸ”´):** Financial impact >$500K per action, irreversible or affects financial statements

**Examples:**
- ðŸŸ¢ Auto-send payment reminder email (no financial transaction)
- ðŸŸ¡ Auto-approve purchase order â‰¤$5K (reversible, bounded impact)
- ðŸŸ  Auto-adjust inventory valuation (affects COGS, margin reporting)
- ðŸ”´ Auto-post revenue recognition journal entries (affects financial statements, audit exposure)

#### Risk Dimension 2: OPERATIONAL CASCADE RISK

**Definition:** The potential for autonomous failure to trigger cascading failures in dependent systems or processes.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Low (ðŸŸ¢):** Failure contained to single transaction, no downstream impact
- **Moderate (ðŸŸ¡):** Failure affects 1-2 downstream processes, recoverable
- **High (ðŸŸ ):** Failure cascades across 3+ processes, requires coordinated recovery
- **Critical (ðŸ”´):** Failure cascades system-wide, affects customer fulfillment or regulatory compliance

**Examples:**
- ðŸŸ¢ Auto-route fulfillment to warehouse A vs B (localized decision)
- ðŸŸ¡ Auto-reorder inventory (affects procurement, inventory, potentially cash flow)
- ðŸŸ  Auto-allocate inventory (affects fulfillment, customer promises, revenue recognition)
- ðŸ”´ Auto-adjust credit limits (affects orders, fulfillment, AR, collections, customer relationships)

#### Risk Dimension 3: REGULATORY / COMPLIANCE RISK

**Definition:** The potential for autonomous action to violate legal, regulatory, tax, or audit requirements.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Low (ðŸŸ¢):** No regulatory impact, purely operational decision
- **Moderate (ðŸŸ¡):** Indirect compliance impact, auditable but low risk
- **High (ðŸŸ ):** Direct compliance impact, requires documentation and justification
- **Critical (ðŸ”´):** Violates regulatory requirements, creates legal exposure, audit failure risk

**Examples:**
- ðŸŸ¢ Auto-prioritize fulfillment queue based on ship date (operational only)
- ðŸŸ¡ Auto-generate draft journal entries for review (audit trail preserved)
- ðŸŸ  Auto-adjust tax classification for products (tax compliance risk)
- ðŸ”´ Auto-post payroll without approval (labor law, tax withholding, audit risk)

#### Risk Dimension 4: REVERSIBILITY RISK

**Definition:** The difficulty and business impact of reversing an autonomous action if it proves incorrect.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Low (ðŸŸ¢):** Easily reversible within minutes, no business impact
- **Moderate (ðŸŸ¡):** Reversible within hours/days, minor business impact
- **High (ðŸŸ ):** Reversible but requires cross-functional coordination, material impact
- **Critical (ðŸ”´):** Irreversible or reversal creates legal/financial harm

**Examples:**
- ðŸŸ¢ Auto-send notification (can send correction, low impact)
- ðŸŸ¡ Auto-create purchase order (can cancel before supplier ships)
- ðŸŸ  Auto-ship order to customer (can recall but costs shipping, customer trust impact)
- ðŸ”´ Auto-pay supplier (funds transferred, requires supplier cooperation to reverse)

### 1.2 Composite Risk Score

Each capability receives a **composite risk score** based on the highest risk dimension:

**Composite Risk = MAX(Financial Risk, Cascade Risk, Compliance Risk, Reversibility Risk)**

**Rationale:** A capability is only as safe as its highest-risk dimension. If any dimension is Critical (ðŸ”´), the capability is Critical risk overall.

**Approval Authority:**
- Board Audit Committee approval required

**Examples:**
- Journal entry posting

### 5.2 Performance Measurement Requirements

To qualify for conditional autonomy escalation, the capability must demonstrate:

#### Criterion 1: Historical Accuracy

- **Measurement:** Compare autonomous recommendation to actual human decision
- **Threshold:** Agreement rate must be >95% (>98% for financial capabilities)
- **Period:** Continuous measurement over minimum 12 months (18-24 months for high-risk)
- **Data:** Minimum 500 decisions (1,000 for financial capabilities)

#### Criterion 2: Override Rate

- **Measurement:** Percentage of autonomous actions overridden by humans
- **Threshold:** <5% override rate (<3% for financial capabilities)
- **Investigation:** If override rate >5%, investigate root cause before escalation
- **Learning:** System must not repeat patterns that lead to overrides

#### Criterion 3: Stable Performance

- **Measurement:** Performance variance over time
- **Threshold:** Accuracy variance <5% quarter-over-quarter
- **Drift detection:** If accuracy degrades >10% from baseline, immediately downgrade autonomy
- **Seasonal adjustment:** Account for seasonal patterns in performance assessment

#### Criterion 4: No Material Failures

- **Measurement:** Count of autonomy failures requiring rollback or causing business harm
- **Threshold:** Zero critical failures, <3 moderate failures per year
- **Definition of failure:** Action that required executive escalation or caused financial/compliance harm
- **Failure review:** Any failure triggers 90-day autonomy freeze

### 5.3 Conditional Autonomy Approval Process

**Step 1: Performance Evidence Submission**
- Operations team submits evidence of meeting all 4 criteria
- Include dashboard showing accuracy, override rate, stability, failure count
- Minimum 12-month performance history required

**Step 2: Risk Assessment Review**
- CTO + Chief Risk Officer assess current risk classification
- Confirm safeguards are implemented and tested
- Validate rollback strategy is operational

**Step 3: Executive Approval**
- COO + CTO + CFO review proposal
- Approve or deny based on risk tolerance and business need
- Document decision rationale

**Step 4: Pilot Period**
- Enable autonomy for 90-day pilot with enhanced monitoring
- Daily performance review for first 30 days
- Weekly executive reporting

**Step 5: Full Activation or Downgrade**
- If pilot successful (criteria maintained), approve full activation
- If pilot underperforms, downgrade to previous tier
- Document lessons learned

## SECTION 7: CONTINUOUS RISK MONITORING

### 7.1 Automated Risk Monitoring

All active autonomous capabilities must have **continuous automated monitoring** to detect risk escalation:

#### Monitor 1: Performance Drift Detection

**Metric:** Autonomous decision accuracy compared to baseline

**Threshold:**
- **Warning:** Accuracy drops >5% from baseline
- **Critical:** Accuracy drops >10% from baseline

**Action:**
- Warning: Daily review by domain owner, investigate root cause
- Critical: Automatic downgrade to lower autonomy tier, executive escalation

**Frequency:** Measured daily, reviewed weekly

---

#### Monitor 2: Error Amplification Detection

**Metric:** Consecutive errors or repeated failure patterns

**Threshold:**
- **Warning:** 3 consecutive errors of same type
- **Critical:** 5 consecutive errors or error rate >5% over 7 days

**Action:**
- Warning: Pause autonomy for investigation
- Critical: Immediate autonomy suspension, root cause analysis required

**Frequency:** Real-time monitoring, alert on threshold breach

---

#### Monitor 3: Override Frequency Tracking

**Metric:** Percentage of autonomous actions overridden by humans

**Threshold:**
- **Baseline:** <5% override rate (healthy autonomy)
- **Warning:** Override rate 5-10%
- **Critical:** Override rate >10%

**Action:**
- Warning: Investigate override reasons, tune logic
- Critical: Downgrade to Level 1 (recommendation only), revise decision model

**Frequency:** Measured weekly, reviewed monthly

---

#### Monitor 4: Business Impact Deviation

**Metric:** Autonomous actions producing worse outcomes than manual baseline

**Threshold:**
- **Warning:** Outcome 5-10% worse than baseline (e.g., higher stockout rate)
- **Critical:** Outcome >10% worse than baseline

**Action:**
- Warning: Enhanced monitoring, investigate contributing factors
- Critical: Immediate autonomy suspension, revert to manual control

**Frequency:** Measured weekly, reviewed monthly

---

#### Monitor 5: Cascade Effect Detection

**Metric:** Autonomous action in one domain triggering unintended consequences in another

**Threshold:**
- **Warning:** Cross-domain impact detected (e.g., auto-reorder causes cash flow alert)
- **Critical:** Cross-domain cascade causing business disruption

**Action:**
- Warning: Review cross-domain dependencies, implement additional safeguards
- Critical: Suspend all cross-domain autonomy, require explicit authorization

**Frequency:** Real-time monitoring via Phase 2.0 control loop correlation

---

### 7.2 Automatic Downgrade Triggers

Autonomy must be **automatically downgraded** (no human approval required) when:

| Trigger Condition | Downgrade Action | Recovery Process |
|-------------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Accuracy drops >10%** | Level 3 â†’ Level 2 or Level 2 â†’ Level 1 | 90-day performance recovery + executive reapproval |
| **Error rate >5% over 7 days** | Immediate suspension (Level 0) | Root cause fix + 30-day pilot + executive reapproval |
| **Override rate >10%** | Level 3 â†’ Level 1 | Logic revision + 90-day testing + executive reapproval |
| **Business outcome >10% worse** | Immediate suspension (Level 0) | Manual control indefinitely, autonomy requires new business case |
| **Cascade effect detected** | Suspend all affected domains | Cross-domain risk assessment + explicit reapproval |
| **Audit trail failure** | Immediate suspension (system-wide) | Audit system restoration + executive + auditor sign-off |

**Downgrade authority:** System automatic OR Domain Owner OR CTO (any can trigger)

**Recovery:** Autonomy cannot be re-enabled until performance recovery is proven and executive reapproval granted

## SECTION 8: INCIDENT RESPONSE FOR AUTONOMOUS FAILURE

### 8.1 Definition of Autonomy Incident

An **autonomy incident** is any event where autonomous action causes or contributes to:

- **Financial harm** >$10K
- **Compliance violation** (regulatory, audit, legal)
- **Customer impact** (SLA breach, incorrect billing, service disruption)
- **Cascade failure** affecting >3 business processes
- **Data integrity issue** (incorrect financial/inventory data)

**Severity Levels:**

| Severity | Financial Impact | Compliance Impact | Customer Impact | Response Time |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Minor** | <$10K | No violation | <10 customers | 24 hours |
| **Moderate** | $10K-$100K | Potential violation | 10-100 customers | 4 hours |
| **Major** | $100K-$500K | Actual violation | >100 customers | 1 hour |
| **Critical** | >$500K or financial statement impact | Regulatory filing impact | Business continuity risk | Immediate |

---

### 8.2 Mandatory Response Timeline

#### Critical Incident (Immediate Response)

**T+0 (Immediate):**
- Autonomy kill-switch activated (all Level 3+ autonomy suspended system-wide)
- CTO, COO, CFO notified immediately
- Incident commander assigned (CTO or designated)

**T+15 minutes:**
- Root cause investigation initiated
- Scope assessment (how many actions affected, financial exposure)
- Rollback initiated for affected actions

**T+1 hour:**
- Executive briefing (CTO â†’ COO/CFO/CEO)
- Customer impact assessment and communication plan
- External audit notification (if compliance impact)

**T+4 hours:**
- Preliminary root cause identified
- Remediation plan drafted
- Board Audit Committee notified

**T+24 hours:**
- Full post-mortem completed
- Autonomy restoration plan or permanent suspension decision
- Board reporting prepared

---

#### Major Incident (1-hour response)

**T+0 (Immediate):**
- Affected autonomy domain suspended
- Domain owner + CTO notified

**T+1 hour:**
- Root cause investigation initiated
- Scope assessment
- Executive notification (CTO â†’ COO/CFO)

**T+4 hours:**
- Rollback completed
- Preliminary findings documented
- Remediation plan drafted

**T+24 hours:**
- Post-mortem completed
- Autonomy recovery plan or downgrade decision

---

#### Moderate Incident (4-hour response)

**T+4 hours:**
- Affected capability suspended
- Investigation initiated
- Domain owner assesses impact

**T+24 hours:**
- Root cause identified
- Remediation implemented
- Performance monitoring enhanced

---

### 8.3 Executive Notification Rules

**Immediate notification required (phone call, not email):**
- Critical incidents (all executives: CEO, COO, CFO, CTO)
- Major incidents (COO, CFO, CTO)
- Any incident affecting financial statements (CFO + external auditors)
- Any incident with regulatory impact (CFO, General Counsel, regulators)

**24-hour notification (email + dashboard):**
- Moderate incidents (COO, CFO, CTO)
- Minor incidents (CTO, Domain Owner)

**Board notification:**
- All Critical incidents (within 24 hours)
- Major incidents with compliance impact (within 48 hours)
- Summary of all incidents in quarterly board report

---

### 8.4 Post-Mortem Requirements

**Mandatory for all Major and Critical incidents:**

**Post-Mortem Report must include:**
1. **Incident Timeline:** When detected, when responded, when resolved
2. **Root Cause Analysis:** What caused the failure? (technical, data, logic, governance gap)
3. **Scope of Impact:** Financial harm, customer impact, compliance exposure
4. **Immediate Actions Taken:** Suspension, rollback, customer communication
5. **Preventive Measures:** How will this be prevented from recurring?
6. **Autonomy Decision:** Restore, downgrade, or permanently suspend?
7. **Accountability:** Who owned the autonomous capability? What oversight failed?

**Review and Approval:**
- CTO prepares report
- COO, CFO, Chief Risk Officer review
- Board Audit Committee receives copy (Critical incidents)

**Autonomy cannot be restored until post-mortem is completed and approved.**

---

### 8.5 Autonomy Suspension Protocol

**Immediate Suspension (no approval required):**
- Any team member can trigger kill-switch for Critical incident
- Domain Owner can suspend their domain for Major incident
- CTO can suspend any autonomy at any time

**Suspension Duration:**
- Critical: Minimum 30 days, requires board reapproval to restore
- Major: Minimum 14 days, requires COO/CFO reapproval
- Moderate: Minimum 7 days, requires CTO reapproval

**During Suspension:**
- Revert to manual control (Level 0) or recommendation only (Level 1)
- Enhanced monitoring of manual decisions (measure if humans perform better)
- Root cause remediation and testing
- Performance recovery evidence gathering

**Failure must slow the system down, not speed it up.**

## SECTION 10: REVIEW & EVOLUTION PROCESS

### 10.1 Quarterly Autonomy Risk Review

**Review Scope:** All active autonomous capabilities across all domains

**Review Components:**

#### 1. Performance Assessment

**For each capability, measure:**
- **Accuracy:** Actual vs baseline performance
- **Override Rate:** % of actions overridden by humans
- **Incident Count:** Minor, Moderate, Major, Critical incidents
- **Business Impact:** Quantified benefit or harm

**Outcome:** Maintain, downgrade, or graduate to higher tier

---

#### 2. Capability Graduation (Tier Advancement)

**Candidates for graduation:**
- Capabilities currently at Level 1 or 2 that meet Level 3 criteria
- Capabilities currently at Level 3 that meet Level 4 criteria

**Graduation Requirements:**
- All performance criteria met for minimum period (12-24 months)
- Zero Major or Critical incidents in past 12 months
- Executive sponsor supports graduation
- Enhanced safeguards designed for higher tier

**Approval:** Autonomy Governance Council + required executives per risk level

---

#### 3. Capability Demotion (Tier Downgrade)

**Candidates for demotion:**
- Capabilities with accuracy <95% baseline
- Capabilities with override rate >10%
- Capabilities with 2+ Moderate incidents or any Major/Critical incident
- Capabilities where business context changed (new regulations, business model shift)

**Demotion Decision:** Domain Owner recommends, Autonomy Governance Council approves

**Post-Demotion:** Capability must meet criteria again before re-graduation considered

---

#### 4. Risk Reclassification

**Triggers for reclassification:**
- New regulations affecting compliance risk
- Business model changes affecting financial impact
- Technology changes affecting cascade risk
- Historical failure patterns indicating higher risk than originally assessed

**Process:**
- Chief Risk Officer proposes reclassification
- CTO + Domain Owner review and concur/dissent
- Autonomy Governance Council approves
- If reclassified to higher risk: autonomy tier may be automatically downgraded

## CLOSING STATEMENT

Autonomy is powerful. It accelerates decisions, improves consistency, and enables scale.

But autonomy is also dangerous. It amplifies errors, erodes governance silently, and can destroy trust in days what took years to build.

**The difference between powerful and dangerous is governance.**

This risk assessment matrix exists to ensure that:
- Every autonomous action has been evaluated for risk
- Every capability has explicit safeguards
- Every failure has clear accountability
- Every escalation has a path to resolution

Autonomy without accountability is just **delegation of blame** when things go wrong.

Autonomy without monitoring is just **willful blindness** to accumulating risk.

Autonomy without rollback is just **commitment to failure propagation**.

This framework ensures that **every autonomous action carries a human accountability.**

When the system acts, a human has authorized it. When the system fails, a human is accountable for recovery. When the system improves, a human has validated the improvement.

**Every autonomous action carries a human accountability.**
